Detailed Pivot Table 19

  • Click on custom objects then go to vizlib library. Drag and drop Vizlib Pivot table to the bottom of the sheet and resize it. 
  • Click on "click to get started " on the pivot table and make your selections. 
  • Select DeptPositionEmp under master dimension and move it to rows.
  • Drag recruitment source under fields and drop it under rows.
  • Under master measure drag Employees, Turnover%, Active%, Months Worked, and NPS then drop them under selected measures. Click on create.
  • Go to DeptPositionEmp and RecruitmentSource to unselect include null values.
  • Click on Turnover% under the measures and change the formatting to number and select 12% as the style.
  • Click on Active% under the measures and change the formatting to number and select 12% as the style.
  • Click on Months Worked under the measures and change the formatting to number and select 1000 as the style.
  • Click on NPS under the measures and change the formatting to number and select 1000 as the style.
  • Scroll down and select indicator for the representation. Type 60 under indicator threshold and green (#008000) for the greater than icon color. The less than icon should be red (='#ff0000') and equals should be gray(='#cccccc').
  • Scroll down to font color and paste the expression below there. 
  • If($(vNPS) < 40 ,Red(),If ($(vNPS) > 60, Green(), If($(vNPS) = 40 and $(vNPS) > 60, 'Black')))
  • Select Apply Style in Title Cell. 
  • Click on Appearance and go to General and turn off show titles.
  • Go to Columns/Rows Definition and show all measure titles. Hide the column and row definitions. Select Orchid (#8e477d) for the definition styling .
  • Go to header cells and make the row height 42. Expand the button to the left and horizontal alignment of the cell text should be center. 
  • Wrap the text and select orchid (#8e477d) for cells styling background. Select white for the font color and make the size 15. Select bold for the font style.
  • Go to Rows (left) cells under Header cells, make the expand button position left and align the cells text to the left. Select Elipsis for wrap text.
  • Go to Columns (Right) Cells and change the Cells styling background to stripes and center the alignment. Change the odd cell colors to gray (#D3D6D6).
